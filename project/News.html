  • The #CrowdStrike Outage Cost Delta Air Lines an Estimated $500 Million Delta Air Lines experienced a significant operational disruption due to a CrowdStrike-caused outage on July 19, 2024, resulting in approximately 7,000 flight cancellations over five days.

    The outage led to an estimated $500 million in damages, prompting Delta to pursue legal claims against CrowdStrike and Microsoft.

    The financial impact includes a $380 million loss in revenue for the September quarter, primarily due to refunds and customer compensation. Additionally, the incident incurred $170 million in non-fuel expenses related to customer reimbursements and crew costs, while fuel expenses were reduced by $50 million due to the flight cancellations.





  • Israel - ZeroSeven Hacking Group Offers Full Network Access to Israeli Infrastructure: 80TB of Critical Data at Risk

    ZeroSeven, the same hacking group that recently leaked sensitive information from Toyota, is now selling full network access to servers containing 80TB of data related to Israeli infrastructure.

    According to the post, this access allows entry to a large amount of sensitive data that could put Israel at significant risk, including: - Detailed project information for water, oil, gas, power, and electricity across most areas of Israel and government sectors.

    - SCADA systems. - Sensitive diagrams with specific coordinates for all projects, pipelines, and more. - Complete databases (customer, financial, sensitive data, etc.). "We can do the same thing we did with Toyota and leak all the data, but instead, we are selling access to the servers so you can do whatever you want, whether it's ransomware attacks, causing damage, or stealing the data. We also offer to delete all data we have uploaded to our servers," the group wrote. It is still unknown whether this claim is true, but if it turns out to be legitimate and falls into the wrong hands, the potential severity of the impact would be critical.
